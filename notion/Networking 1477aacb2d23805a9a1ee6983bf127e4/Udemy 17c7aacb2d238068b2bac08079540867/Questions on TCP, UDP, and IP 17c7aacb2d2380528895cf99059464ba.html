Questions on TCP, UDP, and IP

TCP Questions and Answers

  1. Q: What does TCP stand for?

    A: Transmission Control Protocol.

  1. Q: Is TCP connection-oriented or connectionless?

    A: Connection-oriented.

  1. Q: What is the purpose of the three-way handshake in TCP?

    A: To establish a reliable connection between the sender and receiver before transmitting data.

  1. Q: How does TCP ensure data reliability?

    A: Through acknowledgments, retransmissions, and error-checking.

  1. Q: Name an example of an application that uses TCP.

    A: HTTP (web browsing) or SMTP (email).

  1. Q: How does TCP handle out-of-order packets?

    A: It reassembles them in the correct order before delivering them to the application.

UDP Questions and Answers

  1. Q: What does UDP stand for?

    A: User Datagram Protocol.

  1. Q: Is UDP connection-oriented or connectionless?

    A: Connectionless.

  1. Q: Why is UDP faster than TCP?

    A: UDP has minimal overhead because it doesn’t establish connections or guarantee delivery.

  1. Q: Does UDP provide error recovery?

    A: No, UDP only performs basic error checking via checksums.

  1. Q: Name an application where UDP is preferred.

    A: Video streaming or online gaming.

  1. Q: What happens if a UDP packet is lost?

    A: The packet is simply dropped, and no retransmission occurs.

IP Questions and Answers

  1. Q: What does IP stand for?

    A: Internet Protocol.

  1. Q: What are the two versions of IP currently in use?

    A: IPv4 and IPv6.

  1. Q: What is the primary function of IP?

    A: Addressing and routing packets across networks.

  1. Q: What is the size of an IPv4 address?

    A: 32 bits.

  1. Q: What is the size of an IPv6 address?

    A: 128 bits.

  1. Q: What does MTU stand for in the context of IP?

    A: Maximum Transmission Unit.

  1. Q: How does IP handle packets that are too large for the network?

    A: It fragments the packets into smaller pieces to fit the MTU.

  1. Q: What is the purpose of an IP address?

    A: To uniquely identify devices on a network.

Mixed TCP, UDP, and IP Questions and Answers

  1. Q: Which protocol is used for streaming services, TCP or UDP?

    A: UDP.

  1. Q: What transport protocol would you use for an application that requires reliability?

    A: TCP.

  1. Q: Which version of IP allows for a significantly larger address space?

    A: IPv6.

  1. Q: Why doesn’t UDP guarantee delivery of packets?

    A: Because it prioritizes speed over reliability.

  1. Q: Can TCP and UDP work with both IPv4 and IPv6?

    A: Yes.

  1. Q: What role does IP play in the TCP/IP model?

    A: It provides addressing and routing for data packets.

  1. Q: What happens if an IP packet cannot reach its destination?

    A: An ICMP (Internet Control Message Protocol) message, such as "Destination Unreachable," may be sent back to the source.

  1. Q: Which protocol uses port numbers to distinguish between different applications?

    A: Both TCP and UDP.
