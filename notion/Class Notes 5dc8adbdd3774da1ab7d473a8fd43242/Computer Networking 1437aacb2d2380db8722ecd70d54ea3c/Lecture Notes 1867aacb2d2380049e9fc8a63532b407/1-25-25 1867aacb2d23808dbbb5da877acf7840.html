Notes on Chapter 1

Approach is that the Internet is a fabric of the business that supports the functions of the business. How do we look at the network as a cohesive unit?

TCP/IP is the predominant architecture today - 20/30 years ago there were a lot more

A “nuts and bolts” view - the hardware running to make the internet

The billions of computing devices connected to each other are called hosts/end systems, running network apps at the internet’s edge

Packet Switches: forward packets (chunks of data) from applications to destinations - routers, switches

Communication links - fiber optics, copper, radio, satellite

Transmisson Rate is also known as bandwidth: how many bits can we push through a given link at a period of time?

Networks - a collection of devices, routers, and links managed by an organization

Network of Networks - interconnected ISP running ‘protocols’ that define the sending and receiving of messages - HTTP or something proprietary like Skype

Internet Standard - RFC (Request for Comments) - Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)

The Internet as a Service View - the internet is an infrastructure that provides services to applications, similar to USPS:

The service view provides programming interface to distributed applications: there are hooks allowing sending/receiving apps to ‘connect’ to, use Internet transport service

provides service options like Web, streaming, video, multimedia, teleconferencing, email, games, e-commerce, social media, inter-connected appliances, etc.

What is a Protocol?

Human protocol - what’s the time, I have a question, introductions. They define a way humans interact. A specific message sent and how that message is received, or other events

Protocols define how computers, potentially running different OSes, interact

Protocols define the format, order of messages sent and received among network entities, and actions taken on msg transmission, receipt

For a TCP transaction, there is a 3-way handshake before the transaction occurs

Network Edge

Hosts: clients and servers

Servers are often in data center

Access networks, physical media: wired or wireless communication links

Network Core: interconnected routers, network of network of networks

These routers are extremely fast and expensive, not like the home router

Routers sitting at the edge are different than those sitting at the core

Routers at the edge need to accommodate a number of hosts

Routers in the core are designed to take an incoming packet, understand where it is going, and push it out

How do we interconnect them?

Need an ISP to connect edge devices to edge routers

When looking for residential ISPs, we want to know the transmission rate (bits per second) of the access network and shared or dedicated access among users

cable-based access

Frequency Division Multiplexing (FDM): different channels are subdivided into different frequencies and put on the same wire. They don’t interfere.

Access is shared as multiple households are using the same wire from cable company

There are different kinds of frequencies/electrical wiring

Hybrid Fiber coax (HFC) asymettric up to 40 Mbps to 1.2 Gbps downstream, 30-100 upstream transmission rate

Network of ccable, fiber attaches homes to ISP router

The more people you have, the slower the traffic is going to be

Digital Subscriber Line (DSL)

voice and data transmitted at different frequencies over dedicated line to central office, separated by a splitter

The wire was dedicated directly

Use existing telephone line to central office DSLAM

data over DSL phone line goes to Internet

voice over DSL phone line goes to telephone net

What is better? Shared access (Cable) or Dedicated access (DSL)?
