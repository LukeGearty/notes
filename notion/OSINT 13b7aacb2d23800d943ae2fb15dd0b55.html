My process for setting up anonymous sockpuppet accounts.

This is my process for setting up an anonymous sockpuppet account.

  1. Come up with a persona for the sockpuppet account.
  1. Use Fake Name Generator to create a person whom you feel fits your sockpuppet persona.
  1. Use This Person Does Not Exist to generate an image. Make sure you inspect the image closely and get one that doesn't have any obvious flaws, as they often do. It is worth picking up some Photoshop, GIMP, Affinity Photo or Designer, or other basic image manipulation skills to fix them and change the background of the image.
  1. Get a burner phone, completely wiped and fresh. Can be any brand that will accept a Mint Mobile SIM card.
  1. Get a burner credit card from Privacy.com to use for on Amazon and possible the Mint Mobile setup. They might need it to set up the account.
  1. Set up a burner Amazon account. We're only going to use it once.
  1. Buy two Mint Mobile SIM cards. You can find them various places online and in stores near you, but you can get two of them for $5 on Amazon. They also give you 1 week free trial with something like 100 text messages, which we're going to use. This gives you two cards for two sockpuppet accounts for only $5.
  1. I like to use Amazon to have the card sent to an Amazon pickup box, which can be anonymous.
  1. Get a VPN that you can set to the physical area in which you want your sockpuppet to "exist."
  1. Set up the Mint Mobile trial account somewhere away from your home; as far as you're willing to go.
  1. Use this Mint Mobile trial phone number to set up all of the websites you need.
  1. I recommend at least set up a Google account and Protonmail account. Both will come in handy at different times.
  1. Once you've set up all the accounts with your trial Mint SIM, set up 2FA on all of the accounts.
  1. After setting up 2FA on all of the accounts, change the phone number to one you have more permanent access to, such as MySudo or Google Voice.
  1. Make sure everything works!
  1. Destroy the SIM card.
  1. Wipe the phone.

A lot of these websites are blocking MySudo, Google Voice, and other VoIP numbers. That's why we go through the Mint phone number first.

They should be less stringent now.

EXIF Data

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) data is metadata embedded within digital photos or other media files that stores information about how, when, and where the file was created. It's commonly found in images taken by digital cameras or smartphones.

Some of the details EXIF data may include:

  • Camera settings: Shutter speed, aperture, ISO, flash settings, white balance, etc.
  • Device details: Camera or smartphone model, lens type, and sometimes the firmware version.
  • Timestamp: Date and time the image was taken.
  • Location: GPS coordinates if location services were enabled (useful for tracking where the photo was taken).
  • Orientation: Whether the image is landscape or portrait.

EXIF data can provide valuable context but can also raise privacy concerns if it exposes sensitive information, like location data. For security and privacy reasons, people sometimes strip EXIF data from images before sharing them online.

When doing email OSINT, keep these in mind:

1. Legality and Ethics

  • Know the Laws: Ensure that the OSINT methods you use comply with applicable laws and regulations, such as privacy laws. Avoid unauthorized access or any illegal activities.
  • Respect Privacy: Only gather publicly available information, and avoid data that the owner has not consented to be public.
  • Disclosure and Intent: If working professionally, be transparent with clients about the techniques you’ll be using and the type of information being collected.

2. Identify the Source and Verify Information

  • Email Format: Verify the structure of the email address, looking for common signs of illegitimacy, such as random strings or misspellings.
  • Verify Data Points: Cross-check data from multiple sources to ensure accuracy. OSINT data can sometimes be outdated or incorrect, so correlating findings from different platforms is critical.
  • Metadata Analysis: If you have access to an email from the address, check its headers to extract information such as IP addresses, which can reveal geolocation and sender details.

3. Assess Potential Social Media and Data Breach Links

  • Social Media Accounts: Email addresses are often associated with social media profiles. Use OSINT tools or manual searches to locate public profiles connected to the email.
  • Data Breaches: Check if the email has been part of any data breaches. Breach data can reveal important information like usernames, passwords, or other associated emails.
  • Password Reuse: For red team assessments or threat analysis, note that individuals often reuse passwords across accounts. Data breaches may indicate the likelihood of this vulnerability.

4. Use of OSINT Tools and Methods

  • Email Lookup Tools: Use reputable tools like Hunter.io, EmailRep, or Sherlock to uncover social profiles, location data, and more. Tools vary widely in their data sources and reliability, so cross-verify results.
  • Search Engines: Conduct a thorough search using operators (e.g., "email@example.com") to find mentions on forums, blogs, or other indexed websites.
  • WHOIS and Domain Info: For emails associated with domains, check domain registration information, which can provide details about the organization or individual behind it.

5. Understand Metadata and Domain Reputation

  • Domain Age and Reputation: For corporate emails, the age and reputation of the domain can provide insight. Older domains with high reputation scores are more likely to be legitimate.
  • Email Headers: Analyzing headers can provide insights into the sender’s server and location, or even suggest phishing tactics if inconsistencies are found.

6. Document Findings and Avoid Unnecessary Interaction

  • Passive Reconnaissance: Avoid direct interaction with the email address. Sending a message to the address can alert the target or raise suspicion.
  • Documentation: Keep a detailed log of sources and findings. This is especially important in professional or investigative contexts to ensure findings are accurate and traceable.

7. Use Public Tools with Caution

  • Limit Dependence on Free Tools: Many free OSINT tools may have limited or outdated data. For more reliable findings, consider professional tools that aggregate data from verified sources.
  • Protect Your Identity: Use a VPN or other privacy tools when performing OSINT to avoid detection and protect your own information.
