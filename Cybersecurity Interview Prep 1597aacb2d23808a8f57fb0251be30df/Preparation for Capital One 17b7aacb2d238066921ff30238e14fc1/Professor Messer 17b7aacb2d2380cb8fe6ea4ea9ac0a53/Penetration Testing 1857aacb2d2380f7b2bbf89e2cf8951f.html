Overview

  • Penetration Testing: Simulates an attack on systems to identify vulnerabilities by performing actual exploits (unlike vulnerability scanning).
  • Commonly performed at regular intervals as:
    • A security best practice.
    • A mandated activity for certain businesses.
  • NIST Resource: The Technical Guide to Information Security Testing and Assessment provides an overview of pen testing.

Rules of Engagement (RoE)

  • Purpose: Defines the scope, rules, and limitations of the pen test to ensure clarity and prevent disruptions.
  • Key Components:
    • Scope:
      • Systems in scope and out of scope.
      • Specific IP address ranges.
    • Timing:
      • Testing hours (e.g., after business hours).
    • Types of Tests:
      • Physical breaches, internal or external network tests.
    • Emergency Contacts:
      • List of who to contact for queries or issues.
    • Handling Sensitive Information:
      • Protocols for managing and documenting discovered data.

Penetration Test Process

  1. Exploitation:
    • Objective: Exploit known vulnerabilities to gain access.
    • Risks:
      • Exploits (e.g., buffer overflow) may cause system crashes.
    • Common Techniques:
      • Brute force attacks.
      • Social engineering.
      • SQL injections.
      • Buffer overflows.
  1. Post-Exploitation:
    • Use the compromised system to:
      • Move laterally within the network.
      • Establish persistence:
        • Create a backdoor.
        • Add/change user accounts.
    • Pivoting:
      • Use compromised systems as relays/proxies to access additional devices.
  1. Access Retention:
    • Avoid relying on the same exploit to regain access if it gets patched.

Vulnerability Lifecycle

  • CVEs (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures):
    • Publicly listed vulnerabilities.
    • Lifecycle:
      1. Discovery:
        • Researcher identifies the vulnerability.
      1. Notification:
        • Researcher informs the software developer or manufacturer.
      1. Fix Development:
        • Developer updates, tests, and publishes a patch.
      1. Disclosure:
        • Vulnerability and patch details are made public.
    • Timeframe: Weeks or months between discovery and patch release.

Bug Bounties

  • Definition: Rewards for identifying and reporting vulnerabilities.
  • Process:
    • Researcher reports a vulnerability to the developer.
    • Developer fixes the issue and releases a patch.
    • Vulnerability details are disclosed publicly after mitigation.
  • Purpose:
    • Encourages vulnerability discovery and responsible disclosure.
