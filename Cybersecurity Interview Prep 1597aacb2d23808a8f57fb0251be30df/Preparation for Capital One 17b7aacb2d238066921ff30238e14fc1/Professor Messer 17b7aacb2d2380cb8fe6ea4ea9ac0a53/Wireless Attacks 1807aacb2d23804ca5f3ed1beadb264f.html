Wireless Attacks Overview

Wireless networks are vulnerable to various attacks due to their open nature, reliance on shared radio frequencies, and weaknesses in older security protocols.

Common Wireless Attacks

  1. Eavesdropping (Packet Sniffing):
    • Description: Intercepting unencrypted wireless communication to capture sensitive data.
    • Tools: Wireshark, Aircrack-ng, Kismet.
    • Countermeasures: Use WPA3 encryption, HTTPS, and VPNs to secure data.
  1. Rogue Access Points (Evil Twin):
    • Description: An attacker sets up a fake access point (AP) that mimics a legitimate network to capture user credentials.
    • Tools: Wifiphisher, Fluxion.
    • Countermeasures: Educate users to verify network names and avoid connecting to unfamiliar networks.
  1. Man-in-the-Middle (MITM):
    • Description: An attacker intercepts communication between devices to steal or manipulate data.
    • Tools: Bettercap, Ettercap.
    • Countermeasures: Enable mutual authentication, enforce HTTPS, and use certificate pinning.
  1. Deauthentication Attack:
    • Description: Forcing devices off a legitimate AP by sending deauth frames, often to capture reconnection traffic.
    • Tools: Aireplay-ng.
    • Countermeasures: Use WPA3 with Management Frame Protection (MFP).
  1. WPS Attack (PIN Brute-Forcing):
    • Description: Exploiting weaknesses in the Wi-Fi Protected Setup (WPS) protocol to gain network access.
    • Tools: Reaver, Bully.
    • Countermeasures: Disable WPS in router settings.
  1. Jamming and Denial of Service (DoS):
    • Description: Overwhelming wireless channels with interference or requests, disrupting communication.
    • Tools: Jammer devices, MDK3.
    • Countermeasures: Use directional antennas, frequency hopping, and monitor spectrum usage.
  1. Key Reinstallation Attacks (KRACK):
    • Description: Exploiting flaws in WPA2’s four-way handshake to decrypt data.
    • Tools: Custom scripts based on KRACK PoC.
    • Countermeasures: Update firmware to fix WPA2 vulnerabilities or switch to WPA3.
  1. Bluetooth Attacks:
    • Types:
      • Bluejacking: Sending unsolicited messages to devices.
      • Bluesnarfing: Accessing sensitive information.
      • Bluebugging: Taking control of a device.
    • Tools: Bluesnarfer, BTScanner.
    • Countermeasures: Disable Bluetooth when not in use, use non-discoverable mode, and enforce strong pairing codes.

General Wireless Attack Prevention Measures

  1. Secure Configuration:
    • Use strong, unique passphrases.
    • Enable WPA3 or WPA2 with AES encryption.
    • Disable SSID broadcasting if possible.
  1. Network Segmentation:
    • Isolate guest networks from internal systems.
    • Use VLANs for better traffic management.
  1. Regular Monitoring:
    • Deploy wireless intrusion detection systems (WIDS) or wireless intrusion prevention systems (WIPS).
    • Use tools like AirMon-ng and NetStumbler for network audits.
  1. Updates and Patches:
    • Keep firmware and software updated to patch known vulnerabilities.
  1. User Awareness Training:
    • Educate users about phishing, rogue APs, and safe wireless practices.
  1. Device Hardening:
    • Disable unused wireless protocols (e.g., WEP, WPS).
    • Restrict MAC addresses through filtering.

Key Tools for Wireless Security Testing

  1. Aircrack-ng Suite: Comprehensive tool for sniffing, cracking WEP/WPA keys, and monitoring networks.
  1. Kismet: Wireless network detector and intrusion detection system.
  1. WiFi Pineapple: Penetration testing hardware for simulating attacks.
  1. Metasploit: Exploit development and payload delivery platform.
  1. CommView for WiFi: Packet capture and analysis tool.
