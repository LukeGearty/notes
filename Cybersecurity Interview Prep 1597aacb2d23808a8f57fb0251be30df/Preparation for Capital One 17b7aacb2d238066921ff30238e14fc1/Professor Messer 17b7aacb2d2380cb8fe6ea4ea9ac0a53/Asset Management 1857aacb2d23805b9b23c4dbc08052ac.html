Purchasing Process:

  • Formal process involving multiple approvals (IT, purchasing department, management).
  • Starts with the end user identifying a need for goods/services.
  • Budget analysis is conducted to ensure affordability.
  • Negotiation with suppliers determines pricing, licensing terms, and contract details.
  • Final purchase occurs after negotiations are complete.
  • Goods/services delivered with an accompanying invoice.
  • Invoice payment terms vary (e.g., immediate, 30/60 days).

Asset Tracking System:

  • Tracks all company assets through their life cycle.
  • Assets are assigned to individuals for accountability.
  • Categorizes devices (e.g., laptop, desktop, mobile) and type (hardware/software).
  • Helps with tax liability differentiation:
    • Hardware: Capital expense, depreciates over time.
    • Software: Operating expense, doesnâ€™t depreciate.
  • Used for inventory and help desk operations:
    • Tracks device locations and details.
    • Provides technical details for support tickets.
  • Can identify components within devices (e.g., CPU, memory).
  • Asset tags physically label devices for identification and security.

Data Sanitization and Device Reuse:

  • Storage drives need secure deletion before reuse or disposal.
  • Methods of sanitization:
    • Secure deletion: Ensures no data recovery is possible.
    • Physical destruction: Using drills, hammers, shredders, or pulverizers.
    • Degaussing: Electromagnetic deletion, rendering drives unusable.
    • Incineration: Ensures permanent destruction.
  • For large-scale drive destruction:
    • Third-party services provide destruction and certificates of destruction for confirmation.

Data Retention:

  • Mandated by regulations and organizational policies.
  • Examples include retaining emails, financial data, or backups.
  • Policies define specific data types and retention periods.
  • Supports disaster recovery, accidental data deletion recovery, and compliance.
  • Requires proper procedures for handling, storing, and retaining data securely.
