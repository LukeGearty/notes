Cryptography - encrypting data

Is the data really secure? How do you know?

The attacker doesn’t have the combination (the key)

So they break the safe - attacking the cryptography

In Cryptography, the protocols and algorithms are public so that the weaknesses are available to see and experiment with

Birthday Attack - In a classroom of 23 students, what is the chance of two students sharing a birthday

About 50%

To about 30, the chances go up to 70%

In the digital world, this is a hash collision

Found through a brute force

The attacker will generate multiple vrsions of plaintext to match the hashes

If any hashes are the same, a collision will occur

Example of a collision
